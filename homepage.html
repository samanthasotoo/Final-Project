Gun Violence

Gun related violence has always been a problem in not just the United States, but the entire globe. This brings us to the controversy of gun control; it is a day-to-day problem that governments and the general public are trying to combat while trying to stay true to their values. The second amendment of the United States Bill of Rights states “A well regulated militia, being necessary to the security of a State, the right of the people to keep and bear arms, shall not be infringed.” There is an ongoing debate on whether or not the second amendment is still relevant today. Some people are blind to the fact that gun related violence is a reality that needs to be stopped.

Just as of August 1st, 2018 there have been 8,489 gun related deaths, 16,432 gun injuries and 203 mass shootings. These numbers must go down for a safer America, and people need to become aware of the dangers that come with keeping guns at home. Without gun control, people are more prone to danger. However, people argue that the inability to obtain a gun makes them feel less safe, and less protected. Yet, they fail to realize that if they need a gun, they can get it: with the proper license and training.

Gang Violence

Individuals living in communities where violence is heavily present are often in fear of their lives. As a result, many tend to carry a weapon as a form of protection. Unfortunately, this can increase the level of aggression if an altercation were to commence. Carrying a weapon adds fuel to the fire instead of stabilizing peace and preventing violent actions. Although individuals feel like they are protecting themselves and their family, many others can view it as a threat, a threat that needs to be eliminated.

People who have suffered from altercations with a gang are not just victims but are people. Research has shown that these individuals tend to suffer from PTSD(post traumatic stress disorder); this makes them more likely to suffer from depression.Depression can vary in different forms. An individual may have develop of feeling of self loathing from an assault due to gang violence or feeling nothingness, they become part of a cycle; lost with no motivation to improve their situation. Someone who lost a family to gang violence may be deprived of love and go searching for acceptance. If they decide to join a gang, they become part of a cycle that they were once a victim of.

Safety Beyond Policing (New York)

This charity centered right here in New York advocates against the use of New Yorkers tax payer dollars towards hiring NYPD officers who target ccommunities of color. Instead, they demand that this money would be better spent by increasing funcing in these communites in other departments such as education. For more information visit their website: Safety Beyond Policing

GRASP: Gang Rescue And Support Project

This charity is about helping kids and young adults escape gang life and get the support they need.For instance, if a child already had a family member part of a gang, it may be hard to avoid joining that organization. However, this charity enables the individual to have some to speak to who has been through similar circumstances, get medical attention if needed. They are not blind to the fact that violence may be used to influence the individual's decision. Furthermore, if a gang member wants to leave and live a better life, tattoo removals are provided, training for jobs, assistance with education and healing circle where they can clear their conscious regarding their past actions. This charity works with the community and spreads awareness in schools, they are respected for their efforts. Visit their Page for more information and to donate: GRASP
